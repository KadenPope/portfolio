What is Inclusive Design?

Creating sites with inclusive design in mind is a win-win for both users and businesses.

Every decision made in design has the capability to include or exclude users. The process of creating websites that are inclusive, shows a strong commitment for diversity. Inclusive Design is a fluid process with the goal to provide a user experience that includes as many people as possible.

Within the world of programming, inclusive design goes beyond including alt attributes to img tags or having adequate contrast. While these are very important, they are only two of many design choices to consider at each stage of development. As a web designer and developer, the majority of these features are invisible.

Example:

Consider that I am selling t-shirts. After selecting styles, I have reached the check-out. I have to confirm the colour I want, but I must decide by looking through blocks of colour. For most people, this wouldn't be a challenge, but what if I were colour blind. I ultimately may have to give my best guess as to which colour the t-shirt I am selecting is.

There is a simple solution to this. Including the name of each colour in the block of visual colour will make all the difference. This solution is invisible by most users, but is crucial for those who need it.

Why do we need Inclusive Design?

Inclusive design is important for both users and businesses. When businesses are not engaging in this process, potential users are being excluded. This is often not purposeful decision, yet can have a strong impact on the ability for your message to reach others.

Inclusive design is not only for people who have disabilities. Visual representations of information can be used by someone who does not speak the language your site is in. Your elderly parent or grandparent may also need to interact with your website? Will they be using the zoom feature or might their hand be shaking as they try to tap a small link on their iPad?

This creates a challenge for developer and designers, each design decision needs to be considered within the lens of inclusion. The goal is not to fill the site with an overwhelming amount of features. Each site is individual, creating the need for unique features.
